SERVICES

Forbearance

Temporarily reduce or suspend your payments, which lets you catch up with your mortgage payments. This must be immediately followed by either a repayment plan or modification.

This gives you time to address your financial difficulties and can be less damaging to your credit score than foreclosure.

Repayment Plan

Get back on track by spreading out your past due balance over several payments in addition to making your regular payments.

This is a temporary arrangement that can be less damaging to your credit score than foreclosure.

Modification

Change the terms of your mortgage permanently to make it more affordable, including changes to the interest rate, loan balance, or loan term.

Whether your financial difficulties are short- or long-term, you should start exploring your options today. Even if you haven’t yet missed a mortgage payment but are worried you might fall behind soon, now’s the time to take action

ADVICE

Send Extra Money to Pay Down Principal

Here's how it works. If you take out a $200,000 30-years Mortgage at an interest rate a 6%, and hold it to term, you'll pay a total of $382,537.97 for your home, including interest of $182,537.97. However, if you send in just $100 each month in additional principal, you'll save more than $49,000 in interest over the term of the loan.

Recast Mortgage for Lower Payments

Here’s how it works: After 10 years of paying your 30-year mortgage with a 6% interest rate and a monthly payment of $1,432.86, your balance is $200,000. With a mortgage recast, you contribute an additional $20,000, and have a new principal amount of $180,000, with the same remaining 20 years to pay it off at 6%. However, your new monthly payment is $1,289.58, for a savings of $143.28 per month.

Refinance Your Loan

The most common way to restructure your loan is with a mortgage refinance, where you replace your current mortgage with a new one at a lower interest rate. If you took that same $200,000 balance on your 6% mortgage and refinanced into one with a 5% interest rate, you’d reduce your monthly payment from $1,199 to $1,074, saving $125 monthly.

Refinancing may be challenging to get approved for in a tight lending environment, where you need stellar credit scores and a steady job history. You’ll also need to pay closing costs, which can run 3% to 6% of the loan amount.

GUIDE

What is Mortgage Refinancing?
A refinancing trasaction happens when you swap out an old loan for a new (better) one. Benefits of Refinancing
• Lower your monthly payment.
• Lower lifetime interest cost.
• Reduce risk, if you have and adjustable rate mortgage.
• Get cash out for other purposes.
• Consolidate debt and possibly get tax benefits.
Cost to Refinance
Mortgage refinancing is not free, you'll pay fees to your new lender to compensate them for offering the loan.
When it's Best Time to Refinance
Mortgage refinancing is a good idea when you'll truly benefit from a new loan.
• Interest rates are low.
• Your credit has improved since you got your first loan.
• You will keep the loan for a long time.
